Visiting a national park is one of the easiest ways to enjoy the great outdoors. These parks offer campsites, trails, maps and other resources to make your trip hassle-free. With 421 national parks in the US, there’s no shortage of public land to explore.

Click on a yellow map marker to learn more about each of the top 10 most visited national parks in the US.

Most visited national parks in order

  1. Great Smoky Mountains National Park – This Tennessee park is known for its four seasons and abundance of wildlife. According to the National Park Foundation, over 17,000 species have been discovered within the park.
  2. Grand Canyon National Park – Helicopter tours, mule rides and rafting are just a few of the activities that make the Grand Canyon stand out. Carefully planning your trip to this Arizona park is a must to make the most of your time, as several excursions and locations require a reservation.
  3. Rocky Mountain National Park – More than 100 peaks in this Colorado park rise above 11,000 feet, according to Estes Park Colorado’s website. Currently facing forest fires, RMNP is in dire need of more snow.
  4. Zion National Park – “Zion” is an ancient Hebrew word from the Bible that means sanctuary or refuge. Mormon pioneers, who were the first Anglo-European settlers to arrive in Southern Utah, named the region.
  5. Yosemite National Park – You may recognize this California park from the 2018 National Geographic documentary Free Solo. Alex Honnold was the first person to climb Yosemite’s El Capitan completely untethered.
  6. Yellowstone Mountains National Park – With more geysers and hot springs than any place on Earth, this Wyoming park is worth a visit (nationalparks.org). The park sits on a volcano… but fear not! It’s dormant.
  7. Acadia National Park – One of the few to 10 parks located on the east coast, Acadia National Park sits on the coast of Maine near Bar Harbor. If you’re looking to escape the heat, Acadia is the place to go. The average summer temperature there is 67 degrees.
  8. Grand Teton National Park – The iconic sharp-top mountains of Grand Teton National Park in Wyoming are hard to mistake. The park is known for its rich history. Evidence suggests humans used the area as long as 11,000 years ago, according to Travel Wyoming.
  9. Olympic National Park – Located on the coast of Washington, Olympic National park is home to both mountains and ocean. According to the National Park Service, “the park protects one of the largest remaining blocks of old growth forest and temperate rain forest in the lower 48 states.”
  10. Glacier National Park – There’s no shortage of blue lakes and ice-capped peaks in Montana’s Glacier National Park. A paddleboard or canoe is a must-have for your trip.
